Customer Journey Map
Family Homeowner

From rodent panic to long-term prevention

How the Anticimex lifecycle transforms a stressed family into a confident, protected household — with detailed customer perspective: feelings, actions, interactions, painpoints, and improvements at every stage.

🏠

The scenario

A homeowner with two young children and a dog has noticed signs of rodents. The situation is causing stress and concern about hygiene and safety. They want it solved quickly, but need confidence the solution is safe for their family.

Emotional state

  • Anxious about children’s health
  • Worried about contamination in the kitchen
  • Concerned the dog might encounter poison
  • Embarrassed — feels like a cleanliness failure
  • Urgency: wants this resolved today, not next week

What they need

  • Fast response from someone local
  • Clear explanation of what’s happening
  • Reassurance that treatment is family-safe
  • Visible results quickly
  • A plan so this doesn’t happen again

Decision drivers

  • Trust: reviews, certifications, local reputation
  • Safety: child-safe, pet-safe methods
  • Speed: can someone come today or tomorrow?
  • Clarity: what will it cost, what will you do?
  • Prevention: will this stop it from recurring?

03
Conversion

Yes, let’s do this

“I want the problem gone, and I don’t want it back. What’s the plan?”

🛠 The inspection

  • Technician arrives on time, in branded uniform
  • Inspects entry points, droppings, nesting signs
  • Explains findings in plain language, no jargon
  • Shows photos of entry points on their device
  • Identifies root cause: gap around exterior pipes

📄 The proposal

  • Treatment today + quarterly prevention plan
  • SMART monitoring devices for ongoing detection
  • All methods explicitly child-safe and pet-safe
  • Exclusion work to seal the pipe gap entry point
  • Clear pricing, digital contract, e-sign on the spot

“He got down on his knees and showed me the gap around the pipes where they were getting in. No one else even looked there. He said ‘we’ll seal this today’ — and he did. That’s when I knew I wanted the ongoing plan.”

Feelings
  • Relief that a competent person is here
  • Cautious optimism about the plan
  • Price sensitivity — is recurring worth it?
  • Trusting the technician’s expertise
Actions
  • Walking the property with the technician
  • Asking questions about every finding
  • Reviewing the proposal and pricing
  • Signing the digital contract on the spot
Interactions
  • Face-to-face with technician during inspection
  • Digital proposal on technician’s device
  • E-sign and payment processing
  • Scheduling confirmation
Painpoints
  • Technical jargon in the proposal
  • Unsure if recurring plan is necessary vs one-time fix
  • Too many plan options to compare quickly

Improvements

  • Simpler contract language, no jargon

“They explained everything. I actually understood what they were doing and why.”

🛠 Treatment

  • SMART sensors installed in crawlspace and kitchen void

📊 Service report

  • Digital report sent within 1 hour of visit
  • Photos of entry points sealed, devices installed
  • Methods used — all family-safe, no hazardous chemicals
  • Prevention recommendations for the homeowner
  • Next visit date confirmed

“He walked me through everything — showed me the traps are in locked boxes the kids can’t open, explained exactly what the sensors do, and sealed the gap where they were getting in. An hour later I got a report with photos of everything. First time I’ve felt safe in my own kitchen in weeks.”

Feelings

  • Confident the root cause was addressed
Actions

Interactions
  • Face-to-face walkthrough with the technician

Retention

Is it actually working?

“I haven’t seen anything in weeks. Is the prevention plan still worth paying for?”

📈 Ongoing proof of value

  • SMART dashboard shows zero activity — prevention is working

🔒 Why they stay

  • Prevention Score visible on their dashboard — proof it’s working
  • Peace of mind: 24/7 monitoring means no surprises
  • Technician knows the property and its specific risk areas
  • The memory of the initial stress is a strong retention driver

“My husband asked why we’re still paying when we haven’t seen a single mouse. I showed him the dashboard — zero activity, all sensors green. That’s exactly the point. We’re paying so we never have to go through that again.”

Feelings
  • Gradually forgetting the initial urgency

  • Reassured when they check the SMART dashboard
  • Comfortable with the recurring technician
Actions
  • Checking the dashboard occasionally (less over time)
  • Reading seasonal risk alerts from the branch
  • Reviewing the annual Prevention Report

Interactions
  • Quarterly technician visit (familiar face)
  • Seasonal email alerts from the branch

➕ Expanded services

  • Considers termite monitoring after learning the house is 20+ years old
  • Sees the value of building environment services for property health

“During the spring visit, the technician said ‘I noticed some moisture under the house — that can attract pests too.’ He wasn’t trying to sell me anything. He was just telling me what he saw. That’s why I trust him when he suggests something.”

Feelings
  • Trusting — the technician has earned credibility
  • Open to suggestions from someone who knows the property
  • Protective of the whole home and garden
  • Slightly wary of being upsold
Actions
  • Listening to the technician’s recommendation during the visit
  • Researching mosquito control for families with kids
  • Comparing the cost of add-ons vs standalone services
  • Discussing with partner before committing
Interactions
  • In-person conversation during quarterly visit
  • Follow-up email with add-on details and pricing
  • SMART dashboard showing the new crawlspace data
  • Phone call with branch to confirm add-on

  • Unclear what each add-on actually covers in practice
  • Can feel like upselling if not framed as genuine advice

Customer value grows organically because the technician sees the property firsthand and makes relevant recommendations.

09
Recovery

If something goes wrong

“I saw droppings again. I thought this was supposed to be fixed.”

⚠ What could happen

  • A new entry point opens after a plumbing repair
  • Seasonal pressure pushes rodents to find new access routes
  • Customer sees signs before the SMART sensors pick it up

⚡ How Anticimex responds

  • Branch Manager contacted directly — no phone tree
  • Emergency re-service within 24 hours, included in the contract
  • Root cause investigation: find and seal the new entry point
  • Additional SMART sensor placed at the new vulnerability
  • Follow-up call from Branch Manager within 48 hours to confirm resolution

“I was upset. I called the branch and the manager picked up, already knew our address, and said ‘we’ll be there tomorrow morning.’ No excuses, no extra charge. They found a new gap from the plumber’s work and fixed it on the spot. That’s why we stay.”

Feelings
  • Frustrated and betrayed — “I’m paying for prevention”
  • Anxious all over again, especially about the kids
  • Questioning whether the service actually works
  • Relieved when the Branch Manager takes it personally
Actions
  • Calling the branch urgently
  • Checking the SMART dashboard for any alerts
  • Documenting the new signs with photos
  • Expecting immediate action, not excuses
Interactions
  • Direct phone call with Branch Manager
  • Emergency re-service visit within 24 hours
  • Root cause investigation on-site
  • Follow-up call within 48 hours
Painpoints
  • Having to explain the full situation again
  • Feeling like the monitoring system failed
  • Worrying about being charged extra for re-service
  • Loss of confidence in the prevention promise
Improvements
  • SMART should detect and alert before the customer sees signs
  • Branch Manager should already have the full history on screen
  • Re-service guarantee clearly communicated at sign-up
  • Proactive “we noticed something” outreach instead of waiting for the call

Fast, personal recovery reinforces trust. The family stays because Anticimex owned the problem and fixed it.

This family went from panic to prevention in one week. From a one-time emergency to a recurring relationship built on trust, data, and genuine care for their home.
